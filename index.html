Interseting Facts About Camels

  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.
  • There are two types of Camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Interseting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons eyes can swivel 180 degrees.
  • There are over 150 species of chameleons internationally.
  • The name chameleon comes from ancient Greek and Latin and means "ground lion" or "lion on the ground."

Interseting Facts About Cicadas

  • Cicadas only emerge when the ground is 64 degrees.
  • Cicadas are one of the loudest insects(120 decibels).
  • There are two classification of Cicadas:annual and periodical.

Interseting Facts About Pandas

  • Pandas do not hibernate.
  • Pandas eat consistently for 12-14 hours a day.
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca.

Interseting Facts About Pigeons

  • Pigeons scientific name is Columba livia.
  • The fastest recorded speed a Pigeon can fly is 92.5 mph.
  • Pigeons use the sun as a guide and an internal ‘magnetic compass’.

Interseting Facts About Bears

  • Most bears have 42 teeth.
  • Black bears can run at the speed up to 35mph.
  • Bears use around 11 different sounds to communicate.
